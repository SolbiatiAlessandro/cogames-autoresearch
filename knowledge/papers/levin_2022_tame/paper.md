# Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere (TAME)

**Full Title:** Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere (TAME): An Experimentally-Grounded Framework for Understanding Diverse Bodies and Minds

**Author:** Michael Levin

**Year / Venue:** 2022 — *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience*, Volume 16, Article 768201

**DOI:** [10.3389/fnsys.2022.768201](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnsys.2022.768201)

**arXiv:** [2201.10346](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.10346)

## Abstract

Synthetic biology and bioengineering now enable the creation of novel embodied cognitive systems in chimeric architectures combining evolved and designed material and software. These advances require new ways of thinking about and comparing diverse intelligences whose composition and origin are unlike any natural model species.

TAME introduces a non-binary (continuous), empirically-based approach to strongly embodied agency. It proposes that minds exist on a continuum, enabling researchers to recognize and compare intelligences regardless of composition or origin. The framework treats **morphogenesis as an instance of basal cognition**, identifies developmental bioelectricity as a key coordination medium, and explores how multi-scale biological competency enhances evolutionary adaptability.

---

## Introduction

The fields of synthetic biology, swarm robotics, machine learning, and exobiology are generating entities — hybrots, xenobots, chimeric organisms — that challenge traditional categories of natural vs. artificial and minded vs. non-minded.

The dominant assumption that brains made of neurons are the privileged substrate for cognition is increasingly untenable. Research in **basal cognition** has identified intelligent problem-solving in single cells, plant tissues, bacterial swarms, and animal epithelia.

TAME's central challenge: **How do we recognize, study, measure, and compare cognition across systems whose composition and history we cannot assume?**

Guiding principles:
- **Gradualism**: Continuous series of forms from complex minds to proto-cognitive systems — no sharp categorical distinctions
- **No privileged substrate**: Cognition is a property of organization and dynamics, not specific matter
- **Empirical tractability**: Focus on detectable cognitive functions, not metaphysical completeness

## Framework

### Four Hallmarks of a Self (on a Continuum)

1. **Goal-directedness at a scale exceeding component capability**: Pursues goals none of its components can pursue alone
2. **Compound memory**: Owns memories binding separate experiences across time
3. **Credit assignment locus**: Entity to which reward and punishment are meaningfully applied
4. **System-level stress responses**: Unified responses to perturbation absent in isolated components

### The Persuadability Axis

A continuum from:
- **Left (brute-force rewiring):** Simple machines (thermostats) — only changed by physically altering hardware
- **Intermediate:** Homeostatic circuits; learning systems responsive to reward/punishment; systems open to conditioning
- **Right (rational persuasion):** Humans — influenced by argument and reason without physical intervention

The "correct" agency level is determined experimentally by identifying which intervention strategies most efficiently enable behavioral control.

### Problem-Solving Across Multiple Spaces

Intelligence = competency at navigating toward desirable states in **any space**:

| Space | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Behavioral | Locomotion, foraging, avoidance | Animal navigation |
| Physiological | Maintenance of homeostatic variables | Ion balance regulation |
| Transcriptional | Gene expression regulation | Novel toxin response |
| Morphogenetic | Achievement of target anatomical configurations | Organ regeneration |

### Stress as the Glue of Agency

Stress propagation is the binding mechanism creating integrated Selves from components:
- Subunits experiencing stress propagate signals compelling distant components to contribute
- This scales individual cellular homeostasis into anatomical and behavioral coordination
- Provides a biologically grounded account of collective intelligence without a central controller

### Multi-Scale Competency Architecture

Nested competency at multiple organizational levels:
- Molecular networks → cell collectives → organ systems → organisms → populations
- Higher-scale agents **deform the option space** for components, making correct behaviors the locally optimal solutions available to sub-agents

### Bioelectricity as a Cognitive Medium

Before neural tissue evolved, cell groups used **ion channels and gap junctions** for coordination:
- Bioelectric voltage distributions serve as **pattern memory** encoding target morphologies (anatomical setpoints)
- When ion channel manipulations alter voltage patterns in planarian tissue, regeneration produces two-headed worms despite wild-type genetics
- These altered patterns persist through multiple regeneration cycles — constituting stable, rewritable long-term memories
- Bioelectricity enables **pre-neural cognition**: collective goal-directed behavior at the tissue scale

## Key Arguments and Results

### Morphogenesis Is Basal Cognition

- **"Picasso tadpoles":** When craniofacial organs are surgically scrambled, tadpoles rearrange tissues through novel, non-standard paths to achieve normal frog morphology — context-sensitive problem-solving
- **Salamander limb regeneration:** Amputation at any position yields proportionate regeneration that stops precisely when correct proportions are restored — cells maintain and reference a stored anatomical setpoint
- **Cessation at goal:** Morphogenetic processes stop when the target is achieved, just as behavioral goal-pursuit ceases at goal attainment

### The Plasticity of Selves

- **Metamorphosis:** Caterpillars undergo drastic brain remodeling to become butterflies yet retain behavioral memories
- **Planarian memory distribution:** Planarians cut into pieces each regenerate, with memories distributed across regenerated organisms including newly formed brains
- **Ectopic eyes:** Tadpoles with eyes transplanted to their tails successfully learn visual tasks
- **Memory transplantation:** Cognitive memories transferable between individuals, mapped onto new neural tissue

### No Privileged Substrate for Mind

- Cognitive functions demonstrated in single cells, plant tissues, bacterial biofilms, and engineered non-neural systems
- **Xenobots** (living machines from frog cells) and **hybrots** (animal brains controlling robotic bodies) exhibit agency resisting conventional classification
- Genetic information no longer uniquely specifies form when artificial scaffolds determine anatomy

### Evolution's Cognitive Scaling Mechanism

Major evolutionary transitions involve:
1. Applying existing problem-solving capacities to new, larger solution spaces
2. Developing stress-propagation mechanisms binding diverse modules into larger functional Selves
3. Creating information structures (memories, setpoints, preferences) at new organizational scales

Bioelectricity is the key enabling mechanism for this scaling — allowing cell groups to coordinate as cognitive collectives before dedicated neural tissue evolved.

## Implications

**Regenerative Medicine:** Understanding morphogenesis as goal-directed problem-solving encoded in bioelectric pattern memory provides new therapeutic targets. Altering bioelectric setpoints — rather than specifying every developmental step — may enable robust organ regeneration.

**Artificial Intelligence:** Biological scaling principles (stress propagation, multi-scale goal hierarchies, distributed pattern memory) offer a fundamentally different architecture for AGI than current neural network approaches.

**Ethics and Sentience:** If sentience is graded and substrate-independent, entities traditionally excluded from moral consideration may warrant reassessment. The framework calls for empirical investigation of sentience rather than phylogenetic proxies.

---

## Conclusion

TAME provides a unified, empirically grounded framework for recognizing and comparing minds across an expanding space of possible embodiments. Core claims:

1. **Cognition is not binary** — exists on a continuum from homeostatic loops to complex metacognition
2. **No privileged substrate** — cognitive capacity is a property of dynamics and organization
3. **Morphogenesis is cognition** — cells collectively solve the problem of what body to build
4. **Stress propagation** is the binding mechanism creating integrated Selves from simpler agents
5. **Multi-scale competency** — nested goal-hierarchies — is the central feature of biological intelligence
6. **The Self is plastic** — cognitive identity persists across radical substrate remodeling

By recognizing minds as **goal-directed collectives solving problems across multiple scales through stress-mediated coordination**, TAME opens new research programs across evolutionary biology, regenerative medicine, AI, and philosophy of mind.
